# beestgraph Hero Image -- AI Generation Prompts

Three prompt variations for generating the beestgraph hero image. Each targets a different use case and visual register.

---

## 1. Minimalist / Logo Style

**Use case:** App icon, favicon, social media avatar, small-format branding.

**Prompt:**

A minimalist logo design of a stylized geometric bee viewed from above, its body formed from a single hexagonal node at the center of a small knowledge graph. Six thin luminous edges radiate outward from the central hexagon to six surrounding smaller hexagonal nodes, forming a honeycomb-like graph structure. The bee's wings are suggested by two subtle translucent arcs extending from the top-left and top-right nodes. The entire figure is rendered in a single accent color -- warm amber-gold (#e8a317) -- against a deep charcoal-navy background (#1a1a2e). The lines glow faintly, as if drawn with light on a dark surface. No text, no gradients, no shadows. Flat vector aesthetic with a slight luminous quality to the edges. The composition is perfectly centered and square, suitable for use as an app icon at any size. Think: the intersection of a circuit diagram and a honeycomb cell, distilled to its most essential geometry.

**Style references:** Stripe's logomark, Linear app icon, the Node.js hexagon. Clean Swiss design meets bioluminescence.

**Color palette:** Amber-gold (#e8a317) on charcoal-navy (#1a1a2e). Optional: a second accent in soft green (#1e8e3e) for one or two outer nodes to suggest the storage layer.

**Composition:** Square, 1:1 aspect ratio. Central subject fills 60-70% of the frame.

## 2. Hero / Banner Style

**Use case:** README header, landing page hero, Open Graph social preview image.

**Prompt:**

A wide-format digital illustration of a luminous knowledge graph floating in deep space. The graph is dense at the center and sparse at the edges, forming an organic cloud-like shape -- not a rigid grid but a living, breathing network. Hundreds of nodes of varying sizes are connected by thin glowing edges. The largest nodes near the center pulse with warm amber light; mid-sized nodes glow in teal-green; the smallest peripheral nodes shimmer in cool blue-white. Each node is a softly glowing hexagonal cell. Inside a few of the larger hexagonal cells, faintly visible content is suggested: a snippet of code syntax, a paragraph of text, a small image thumbnail, a video play button, a musical note, a recipe card -- representing the diversity of captured knowledge. A single stylized bee, rendered in golden wireframe with translucent wings, traces a curving flight path through the graph. Its trail leaves behind a fading dotted line that connects several nodes, visually representing the act of linking ideas. The flight path has an elegant spiral quality, like a Fibonacci curve. The background is a deep gradient from near-black (#0a0a1a) at the edges to dark navy (#1a1a2e) at the center, with a very subtle radial glow behind the densest part of the graph. Tiny particle-like dots drift in the background space, suggesting stars or data points yet to be captured. The overall mood is contemplative, vast, and quietly alive -- a personal universe of knowledge being mapped in real time. No text overlays. The lighting is entirely from the nodes and edges themselves: self-illuminated, no external light source.

**Style references:** The aesthetic of Kurzgesagt (the cosmic scale and clean glow), combined with the data visualization art of Jer Thorp and the network visualizations of Barabasi Lab. Think also of the opening title sequence of Westworld (glowing wireframes in dark space) but warmer and more organic.

**Color palette:** Primary glow: warm amber (#e8a317, #f5c842). Secondary: teal-green (#1e8e3e, #4ade80). Tertiary: cool blue (#1a73e8, #60a5fa). Background: deep navy-black (#0a0a1a to #1a1a2e). The bee wireframe: pure gold (#fbbf24).

**Composition:** Wide format, 2:1 or 16:9 aspect ratio. The graph cloud is centered horizontally but sits slightly above vertical center, leaving breathing room at the bottom. The bee and its flight path occupy the upper-right quadrant, moving from right to left, drawing the eye inward toward the dense center.

**Technical notes:** High resolution (minimum 2400x1200). The glow effects should feel physically plausible -- bloom on bright nodes, falloff on edges. Avoid neon harshness; the light should feel warm and diffused, like bioluminescence rather than LEDs.

## 3. Conceptual / Artistic Style

**Use case:** Conference talk slide, poster print, the version you frame on the wall.

**Prompt:**

An artistic interpretation of a knowledge ecosystem as a living organism. The scene depicts a vast honeycomb structure growing organically in three dimensions, viewed at a slight angle from above. The honeycomb is not uniform -- it warps and curves like a natural beehive, with some cells larger than others, some broken open, some still being constructed. Each hexagonal cell is a translucent amber membrane stretched over a frame of thin glowing edges. Through the translucent walls, different worlds are visible inside each cell: one contains a miniature library with tiny floating books; another holds a constellation of stars arranged like a neural network; a third shows lines of code cascading like a waterfall; another contains a tree whose branches are circuit traces; one holds a small planet with rings made of text. At the heart of the structure, where the cells are densest and most luminous, a large bee rests -- not a photorealistic insect but a mythic, stylized creature. Its body is made of dark polished obsidian with veins of glowing amber running through it like magma through volcanic rock. Its wings are vast, delicate, translucent structures that resemble graph visualizations -- the wing veins are edges, the intersection points are nodes, and tiny labels float near each node like distant stars. The bee's compound eyes are rendered as two clusters of tiny hexagonal screens, each showing a different piece of information. Fine golden threads extend from the bee's legs down into the honeycomb cells below, connecting to the content within -- the bee as librarian, curator, connector. The lighting comes from within: the amber glow of the cells, the green phosphorescence of the bee's wing-graph, cool blue pinpoints of light from the content worlds inside the cells. The background fades to absolute black, making the structure float in a void. A few detached hexagonal cells drift at the periphery, trailing thin threads back toward the main structure -- new captures being pulled in, not yet integrated. The overall feeling is of something ancient and futuristic simultaneously: a cathedral of knowledge tended by its guardian. Painted in the style of a detailed digital matte painting with influences from James Jean's organic surrealism, Moebius's architectural imagination, and the bio-mechanical aesthetics of Giger but rendered warm rather than menacing.

**Style references:** James Jean (organic flowing forms), Moebius / Jean Giraud (architectural dreamscapes), the bio-luminescent forests of Avatar (the glow quality), Ernst Haeckel's Kunstformen der Natur (the beauty of natural structures rendered with scientific precision), and Remedios Varo (surrealist architecture that feels inhabited and alive).

**Color palette:** Dominant: amber and gold (#e8a317, #fbbf24, #92400e) for the honeycomb structure. The bee's body: obsidian black with amber veins. Wings: teal-green (#1e8e3e) fading to blue (#1a73e8) at the tips. Content worlds inside cells: each has its own accent -- warm whites for text, cool blues for code, greens for nature content, soft reds for media. Background: true black (#000000) to make the structure pop.

**Composition:** Portrait or square format (1:1 or 3:4). The honeycomb structure fills the frame, with the bee at the golden ratio intersection point (roughly upper-left third). Depth of field: the cells nearest the viewer are sharp and detailed; those further away dissolve into soft glowing shapes, suggesting the knowledge base extends far beyond what is visible.

**Technical notes:** High detail. The content visible inside the cells should reward zooming in. The bee should be unmistakably a bee but clearly not photographic -- it is a symbol, a totem, rendered with the reverence of a medieval manuscript illumination but in a contemporary digital painting style.

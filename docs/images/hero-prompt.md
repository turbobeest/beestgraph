# beestgraph Hero Image — AI Generation Prompts

The name is **beest**graph — as in **beast**. The image should evoke a powerful, intelligent, mythic creature whose body is the knowledge graph itself. Not cute, not corporate. A living beast made of connected knowledge.

---

## 1. Minimalist / Logo Style

**Use case:** App icon, favicon, social media avatar.

**Prompt:**

A minimalist logo of a stylized beast head in profile — part wolf, part dragon, part something unnamed and ancient. The head is constructed entirely from graph edges and nodes: the jaw is a bold angular line connecting three nodes, the eye is a single luminous hexagonal node pulsing with amber light, the crown/horns are branching edges that extend upward and fork like a knowledge tree. The beast is facing right, suggesting forward motion. Rendered in warm amber-gold (#e8a317) as a single continuous line-and-node drawing against a deep charcoal-navy background (#1a1a2e). The lines glow faintly with a subtle bloom effect. No fills, no gradients — pure topology. The silhouette should be immediately recognizable as both "beast" and "graph" simultaneously. Centered in a square frame, suitable for any size from favicon to billboard.

**Style references:** The Witcher wolf medallion reduced to its geometric essence. Puma logo meets circuit diagram. Norse knotwork made of graph edges.

**Color palette:** Amber-gold (#e8a317) on charcoal-navy (#1a1a2e). The eye node: bright white-gold (#fbbf24) with bloom.

**Composition:** Square, 1:1. The beast head fills 70% of the frame.

## 2. Hero / Banner Style

**Use case:** README header, landing page hero, Open Graph social preview.

**Prompt:**

A wide-format digital illustration of a massive luminous knowledge graph floating in dark space — but the graph IS a beast. Seen from a slight distance, the overall shape of the node-and-edge network forms the unmistakable silhouette of a powerful creature — shoulders hunched, head lowered, eyes forward, as if stalking through the darkness. Up close, the beast dissolves into hundreds of interconnected nodes of varying sizes connected by thin glowing edges. The largest nodes along the spine and skull pulse with warm amber light. Nodes along the limbs glow teal-green. The smallest nodes at the periphery — the tips of fur, the edge of the tail — shimmer in cool blue-white, trailing off into scattered particles like the beast is still forming, still capturing new knowledge at its edges. The two eye-nodes are the brightest points in the image: intense amber-white, casting faint light onto the nearest edges like headlights in fog. Inside several of the larger body-nodes, faintly visible content is suggested through translucent hexagonal cells: a snippet of code, a paragraph of text, a video thumbnail, a mathematical formula — the knowledge this beast carries within it. The beast's posture is alert but calm — not attacking, but aware. A guardian. Its outline isn't sharp; it emerges from and dissolves back into the graph, as if the boundary between creature and network is fluid. A few disconnected nodes float nearby, trailing thin threads toward the beast — new captures being drawn in, about to become part of the body. The background is near-black (#0a0a1a) with a subtle radial glow behind the beast's core, and faint particle-stars drifting in the void. The mood is powerful, contemplative, vast — a personal beast of knowledge, alive in the dark. No text overlays. All light is self-emitted from the nodes and edges.

**Style references:** The ethereal creatures in Princess Mononoke (the Forest Spirit's presence and luminosity). The particle-based creatures in Annihilation. The dark grandeur of a Beksinski painting but warm rather than nightmarish. The data-sculpture aesthetic of Refik Anadol's machine hallucinations. Think also: if the constellation Ursa Major were actually a bear made of stars and you could see every star as a node in a knowledge graph.

**Color palette:** Primary glow: warm amber (#e8a317, #f5c842) for the core/spine/eyes. Secondary: teal-green (#1e8e3e, #4ade80) for the limbs. Tertiary: cool blue (#1a73e8, #60a5fa) for the periphery/particles. Background: deep void (#0a0a1a to #1a1a2e). Eye nodes: intense white-gold (#fef3c7 center, #fbbf24 bloom).

**Composition:** Wide format, 16:9 or 2:1 aspect ratio. The beast faces left-to-right, positioned slightly left of center. Its head is in the left third, body fills the center, and the dissolving tail/particles trail into the right third. The eye-nodes are at the golden ratio intersection. Leave breathing room at top and bottom — the beast should feel like it's moving through open space, not crammed into a box.

**Technical notes:** Minimum 2400x1200. The glow effects should feel physically plausible — bloom on bright nodes, soft falloff on edges. The beast's form should be unmistakable at thumbnail size (the silhouette reads as an animal) but richly detailed when zoomed in (individual nodes, content inside cells, edge connections). Avoid neon harshness; the light is warm bioluminescence, not LEDs.

## 3. Conceptual / Artistic Style

**Use case:** Conference talk slide, poster print, the version you frame on the wall.

**Prompt:**

A mythic beast of knowledge rendered as a towering digital matte painting. The creature stands on a dark reflective surface — obsidian glass or still water — that mirrors its luminous form below. It is massive, ancient, and impossible: part stag with branching antlers, part wolf with a powerful chest, part something entirely its own. Its body is not flesh but a living, breathing knowledge graph. The skeletal structure is visible through translucent skin — and the bones are thick glowing graph edges, amber-gold, pulsing with data. The muscle fibers are thousands of thinner edges bundled together, teal and green, carrying information between node-joints. Its antlers are the most spectacular feature: they branch upward and outward in a massive tree-like structure, each fork a new edge, each tip a glowing node. The antlers are the topic hierarchy — close to the skull the branches are thick and labeled with broad categories (technology, science, culture), and as they fork outward the labels become more specific (ai-ml, quantum-physics, film) until the outermost tips are individual document nodes, softly pulsing. Some antler-branches are dense with nodes (areas of deep knowledge); others are sparse with only a few scattered lights (areas just beginning to be explored). The beast's eyes are compound — clusters of tiny hexagonal screens like an insect's eye, each facet showing a different piece of captured knowledge: a code snippet, a recipe, a tweet, a face, a formula. The eyes glow with warm amber intelligence. From the beast's chest, where the heart would be, a bright golden light radiates outward through the edge-veins of the body — the core engine, the processing pipeline, the thing that turns raw captures into connected knowledge. Thin golden threads trail from the beast's hooves down into the reflective surface below, connecting to faint node-clusters visible in the reflection — the archive, the history, the diary of bygones. Around the beast, floating at various distances, disconnected nodes drift in the dark air like fireflies or dandelion seeds — captures not yet integrated, knowledge not yet connected, drawn slowly toward the beast by invisible gravity. The overall atmosphere is reverent and awe-inspiring. This is not a monster — it is a guardian, a companion, a second brain given form. It stands in darkness because it IS the light. Painted in a detailed digital matte painting style with influences from Jakub Rozalski (mythic scale with technological undertones), Greg Rutkowski (dramatic fantasy lighting), and the biological-mechanical fusion aesthetic of Tsutomu Nihei's Blame! manga, but warmer and more inviting.

**Style references:** The Patronus charm from Harry Potter (a guardian creature made of light). The deer god from Princess Mononoke (ancient, powerful, luminous). Jakub Rozalski's paintings (mythic creatures in landscapes with technological undertones). The fractal antlers of mathematical art. Ernst Haeckel's biological illustrations (the beauty of natural branching structures).

**Color palette:** The beast's skeleton/core edges: amber-gold (#e8a317, #fbbf24, #92400e). Muscle/body edges: teal-green (#1e8e3e, #4ade80). Antler tips/peripheral nodes: cool blue (#1a73e8, #60a5fa). Eyes: warm white (#fef3c7) with amber bloom. Heart/core glow: bright gold (#fbbf24). Reflection: same palette but dimmer, cooler, slightly blue-shifted. Background: true black (#000000) above the beast, dark reflective surface below. Floating disconnected nodes: pale blue-white (#e0e7ff).

**Composition:** Portrait or square (1:1 or 3:4). The beast stands at center, antlers filling the upper third and extending beyond the frame edges (suggesting the knowledge extends further than what's visible). The reflection occupies the bottom quarter. The floating disconnected nodes are scattered across the mid-ground. The golden heart-glow is at the exact center of the frame.

**Technical notes:** Maximum detail. The antler-branches should reward zooming in — at full resolution, individual node labels should be faintly legible. The compound eyes should have visible content in each facet. The muscle-edge bundles should show individual strand-edges when zoomed. The reflective surface below should have subtle ripples where the golden threads enter it. This image should feel like it could be a real painting hanging in a gallery — it has narrative depth, not just visual complexity.
